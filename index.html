Trading Plan - Intraday Cycles

Strategy based on 3drives cyclical concepts | FEPO (For Education Purpose Only)

General Interest

The objective is to align with institutional movements by identifying market manipulation phases, post (re)accumulation/(re)distribution. The strategy relies on patience, discipline, and waiting for multiple confluences before risking capital. We don't trade patterns; we trade context.

Main Strategy: The Re-accumulation Scenario

This strategy aims to capture the continuation of a major trend after a manipulation phase intended to liquidate "weak hands" (re-accumulation). Simply put, it's a retracement before a new expansion in the direction of the initial trend (IRL > ERL).

1. Bias (HTF)

Identify the underlying trend on the H1. If the trend is bullish, our bias is Bullish. We will only look for buys.

2. Manipulation

Wait for a bearish manipulation that pushes the price into a "Discount" zone (below 50% of a relevant range, ideally at the 0.75 level).

3. Confirmation (LTF)

Observe a clear bullish reaction on the M1/M5 when the price reaches our area of interest. This is the entry signal.

Key Confluences & Filters

The conditions must be met to consider a trade.

  • Time Filter: The setup must occur during Cycle 3 (06:00 - 12:00 EST), the "New York" session, to maximize volatility and liquidity.
  • Price Filter: The entry must be in a Discount zone (for a buy) or Premium zone (for a sale).
  • Point of Interest (POI): The "Discount" zone must contain a confluence of an Order Block (OB) that supports the initial trend and an unmitigated or partially mitigated Fair Value Gap (FVG) in the direction of the trend. This is our high-probability zone.
  • Liquidity Target: A clear and logical target must exist, typically the Previous Day High (PDH) for a buy, or the Previous Day Low (PDL) for a sale.
  • Stop Loss: It represents our invalidation level, typically the Swing(L) that initiated the +FVG for a buy, or the Swing(H) that initiated the -FVG for a sale.

Position Entry Model (The Setup)

The exact sequence to execute a trade.

  1. Hypothesis: Formulate a clear thesis. Ex: "The H1 trend is bullish. The price dropped at the beginning of cycle C1. I anticipate a manipulation in C3 to reach the OB+FVG on H1 before targeting the PDH."
  2. Waiting: Let the price reach the predefined Point of Interest (POI) without action. Patience is key.
  3. Confirmation: Wait for the mandatory confirmation signal on the LTF (M1/M5): a Change in State of Delivery (CISD) or Market Structure Shift (MSS). This manifests as a powerful rejection candle or a break of the local structure to the upside.
  4. Entry: Place the buy order only after the closing of the CISD/MSS confirmation candle.
  5. Invalidation: If the price moves through the POI without reaction or if no clear CISD/MSS forms, the setup is invalid. No trade is taken.

Position Management

Stop Loss (SL)

Placed logically below the low of the Order Block or below the wick of the manipulation/confirmation candle. This level corresponds to an invalidation. After a CISD/MSS, it must remain preserved. If it is broken, we consider it an expansion from a previous manipulation, which cancels our interest in this trade.

Take Profit (TP)

The main target is the opposing liquidity pool, either the PDH when Bullish, or the PDL when Bearish. Partial profits can be considered at intermediate structure levels.

Final Checklist Before Entering

Ensure each criterion is met.

  • Is the H1 trend aligned with my bias?
  • Is the price in a relevant Discount / Premium zone?
  • Are we in Cycle 3 (sC3 / optional)?
  • Has the price touched a clear OB + FVG zone?
  • Have I observed a clear and indisputable CISD/MSS on the LTF?
  • Is the Risk/Reward ratio towards the PDH/PDL favorable (min 1:2)?
  • Have I checked the economic calendar for imminent high-volatility news?
